Off-Plan Property in Dubai
What is off-plan property in Dubai? +
Off-plan property in Dubai refers to properties purchased directly from developers before or during construction. Buyers pay a deposit — typically 10% — and the remainder in instalments tied to construction milestones, often with post-handover payment plans available.
Is buying off-plan property in Dubai safe? +
Yes. Dubai's Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA) protects off-plan buyers through mandatory escrow accounts, ensuring developer funds are only released at verified construction milestones. Dubai is one of the most tightly regulated off-plan markets in the world, making it a highly secure environment for property investment.
What is the minimum deposit for off-plan property in Dubai? +
Most Dubai developers require a minimum deposit of 10% of the property price to secure a unit. Some new launches offer as little as 5% on reservation, with the remaining balance spread across construction milestones and post-handover payment plans that can extend several years beyond completion.
Can foreigners buy off-plan property in Dubai? +
Yes. Non-UAE nationals can freely purchase off-plan property in Dubai's designated freehold zones, which cover the majority of major communities including Downtown Dubai, Dubai Marina, Palm Jumeirah, Dubai Hills Estate, Business Bay, JVC and many more. There is no restriction on nationality.
What is the best area to buy off-plan property in Dubai? +
The best area depends on your budget and goals. Downtown Dubai, Dubai Marina and Palm Jumeirah offer prestige and strong rental demand. Dubai Hills Estate, JVC and Emaar South offer better entry prices with solid capital appreciation. Dubai Creek Harbour and Emaar Beachfront are among the fastest-growing new communities. We can advise you on the right fit for your investment.
